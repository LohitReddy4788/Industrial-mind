STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
==============================
SOP Number: SOP-012
Title: Pressure Vessel Inspection and Entry Procedure
Revision: 2
Date: 10-Mar-2023
Prepared by: Amit Shah (Inspection Engineer)
Approved by: D. Venkataraman (Head of HSE)
Applicable Equipment: All pressure vessels — V-201, V-205, V-301, V-302 series
Regulatory Basis: OISD-118 Section 12, Factory Act Section 31, Factories Act Rules (Gujarat)

1. SCOPE
This procedure applies to statutory and non-statutory internal and external inspection
of pressure vessels. Includes procedures for vessel entry (confined space entry).

2. STATUTORY INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS
Per Factory Act Section 31 and OISD-118 Section 12:
- External inspection: minimum every 2 years
- Internal inspection: minimum every 4 years OR as determined by API-510 inspector
- All inspections must be performed by or under supervision of a competent person
  (API-510 certified or equivalent)
- Third-party inspection required for vessels > 15 years old or where defects found

3. PRE-INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Vessel Isolation:
Step 1: Shutdown and depressure vessel to atmospheric pressure.
Step 2: Drain all process fluid — verify level gauge reads zero.
Step 3: Purge vessel with nitrogen (minimum 3 vessel volumes).
Step 4: Gas test — must achieve: O2 18-21%, LEL <5%, H2S <10 ppm, CO <50 ppm.
Step 5: Issue Confined Space Entry Permit (CSEP) signed by Area Authority.
Step 6: Continuous gas monitoring required during entire entry period.
Step 7: Standby person must be positioned at vessel manway at all times during entry.

3.2 Entry Requirements:
- Minimum 2 persons: 1 inside, 1 standby outside at all times
- Emergency rescue equipment at entry point: self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA), safety rope
- Communication: voice or radio contact every 10 minutes
- Maximum continuous stay inside vessel: 30 minutes (then exit for rest)
- Re-entry only after fresh gas test

4. INSPECTION PROCEDURE

4.1 External Inspection:
Step 1: Visual examination — surface corrosion, paint condition, insulation damage.
Step 2: Nozzle inspection — flange condition, valve condition, PSV condition.
Step 3: Support and saddle inspection — weld condition, corrosion.
Step 4: Thermographic scan if moisture ingress in insulation suspected.
Step 5: Record all findings with photographs.

4.2 Internal Inspection:
Step 1: Enter vessel per Section 3.2.
Step 2: Visual examination of all internal surfaces — identify areas for UT measurements.
Step 3: Wall thickness measurement by UT (Olympus 38DL Plus or equivalent):
   - Minimum 20 measurement points per vessel
   - Higher density in bottom quadrant and corrosion-prone areas
   - Record all readings and compare to minimum required thickness
Step 4: Weld inspection — minimum 10% of all welds by MT or PT
Step 5: Nozzle weld inspection — all nozzles shall be inspected
Step 6: Photograph all significant findings

4.3 Overpressure Protection:
Step 1: Verify PSV set pressure against vessel design pressure.
Step 2: Confirm PSV test records are current (due every 12 months per OISD-118 Section 12.4).
Step 3: If PSV overdue — DO NOT RETURN VESSEL TO SERVICE until tested and certified.

5. FITNESS FOR SERVICE ASSESSMENT
Following inspection:
- Calculate remaining corrosion allowance = actual thickness - required minimum thickness
- Calculate remaining life = remaining corrosion allowance / corrosion rate
- If remaining life < 2 years: escalate to Chief Inspector for fitness for service review
- Determine next inspection date per API-510 methodology

6. DOCUMENTATION
- Complete Inspection Report with all readings and findings
- Issue Inspection Certificate (Form INS-003)
- Third-party certificate (for statutory inspections)
- Submit to Plant Safety Officer within 7 days of inspection
- Update plant inspection database (SAP PM)

7. STATUTORY NOTIFICATION
For significant findings (wall thinning > 30% corrosion allowance consumed):
- Notify Chief Inspector within 24 hours
- For any vessel with imminent risk: shut down immediately, notify Factory Inspector per Factories Act

END OF PROCEDURE
