STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
==============================
SOP Number: SOP-007
Title: Centrifugal Pump Maintenance and Seal Replacement Procedure
Revision: 3
Date: 15-Jan-2023
Prepared by: Priya Menon (Reliability Engineer)
Approved by: D. Venkataraman (Head of HSE)
Applicable Equipment: All centrifugal pumps — P-101, P-102, P-201, P-202 series
Regulatory Basis: OISD-118 Section 6.2, Factory Act Section 31

1. SCOPE
This procedure covers the safe isolation, inspection, maintenance, and recommissioning of
centrifugal pumps. It applies to all planned and corrective maintenance activities.

2. PRE-WORK REQUIREMENTS
2.1 Permit to Work (PTW): A valid Hot Work or Cold Work permit must be obtained from the
    Area Authority before beginning any maintenance activity.
2.2 LOTO (Lock Out Tag Out): Electrical isolation must be completed per LOTO-001.
    Motor must be de-energised and locked at the MCC panel.
2.3 Process Isolation: Suction and discharge valves must be locked closed.
    Vent pump casing pressure to zero. Verify zero pressure with pressure gauge.
2.4 Gas Testing: Confined area gas test required if pump handles hydrocarbons.
    Maximum allowable LEL: 5% (by CGI instrument reading).
2.5 Personal Protective Equipment: Chemical-resistant gloves, face shield, chemical splash goggles,
    safety boots. Anti-static clothing for hydrocarbon service pumps.

3. PUMP ISOLATION PROCEDURE
Step 1: Notify control room and obtain written acknowledgement.
Step 2: Stop pump from local push-button station — confirm DCS shows STOP.
Step 3: Close discharge valve XV-P101-D (or applicable discharge valve).
Step 4: Close suction valve XV-P101-S.
Step 5: Vent pump casing via vent valve on top of casing.
Step 6: Verify zero pressure on PI at pump outlet.
Step 7: Drain pump casing if required — direct drain to closed drain system.
Step 8: Apply LOTO at MCC panel. Complete LOTO record sheet.

4. MECHANICAL SEAL INSPECTION AND REPLACEMENT
Step 1: Remove coupling guard.
Step 2: Check coupling alignment before disassembly — record readings.
Step 3: Remove coupling. Handle coupling halves carefully — do not drop.
Step 4: Remove seal gland plate bolts. Note torque values.
Step 5: Slide gland plate back along shaft.
Step 6: Remove seal assembly carefully. Note orientation for reinstallation.
Step 7: Inspect seal faces — look for: scoring, pitting, chipping, heat stress cracks.
Step 8: Clean seal chamber and shaft. Remove all deposits.
Step 9: Install new seal per manufacturer instructions.
    IMPORTANT: Ensure all O-rings are lubricated with compatible grease before assembly.
    IMPORTANT: Observe correct spring orientation (always towards pump).
Step 10: Reinstall gland plate. Torque bolts in cross pattern to specified torque (see Appendix A).
Step 11: Verify seal flush Plan 11 line is clear — check orifice for blockage.
Step 12: Check coupling alignment — must meet manufacturer specification (typically 0.05mm max).
Step 13: Reinstall coupling guard.

5. BEARING INSPECTION
Step 1: Remove bearing housing end cover.
Step 2: Inspect bearing for: pitting, discolouration (overheating), roughness.
Step 3: Record bearing temperature prior to disassembly if taken under operation.
Step 4: Replace bearing if vibration exceeded 7.5 mm/s or bearing temperature exceeded 70°C.
Step 5: Repack with specified grease — do not overfill (max 60g per bearing).

6. RECOMMISSIONING
Step 1: Remove all LOTO. Restore electrical isolation.
Step 2: Open suction valve fully. Partially open discharge valve (10-15%).
Step 3: Prime pump — open vent valve until water/liquid flows without air.
Step 4: Start pump from DCS. Verify flow and pressure within 2 minutes.
Step 5: Gradually open discharge valve to design flow.
Step 6: Check: vibration <5 mm/s, bearing temperature <60°C, seal leakage: 0 drops/min.
Step 7: Monitor for 15 minutes before handing back to operations.

7. POST-MAINTENANCE DOCUMENTATION
- Complete Work Order and enter in SAP PM.
- Record parts used, measurements taken, and any observations.
- If new failure modes discovered, raise Maintenance Notification for engineering review.

APPENDIX A — Torque Values
Gland plate bolts M16: 75 Nm
Coupling bolts M20: 110 Nm

NOTE: This procedure does NOT currently cover the procedure for testing backup mechanical seals
on dual-seal arrangements. Where dual seals are fitted (P-201 series), contact Reliability
Engineering for supplementary procedure. (Gap noted — revision planned for SOP-007 Rev.4)

END OF PROCEDURE
