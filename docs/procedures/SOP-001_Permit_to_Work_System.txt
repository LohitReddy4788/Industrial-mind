STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP Number: SOP-001
Title: Permit to Work (PTW) System
Revision: 5
Effective Date: 01-April-2023
Approved By: Head of Operations, Head of Safety
Applicable To: All maintenance, inspection, and modification work in plant

SCOPE:
This SOP covers the Permit to Work system for all non-routine work including maintenance, inspection, testing, construction, and modification activities at all plant areas classified as hazardous (Zone 0, Zone 1, Zone 2 as per IS 5572) and non-hazardous.

TYPES OF PERMITS:

1. COLD WORK PERMIT (CWP)
For non-sparking, non-ignition-risk work in non-hazardous areas or after full isolation.
Examples: Instrument calibration in control room, civil work away from process areas, painting
Validity: 8 hours (one shift)
Issuing Authority: Shift Incharge
Approving Authority: Area Supervisor

2. HOT WORK PERMIT (HWP)
MANDATORY for any work involving open flame, spark, or heat generation in or near hazardous areas.
Examples: Welding, grinding, gas cutting, soldering, use of non-intrinsically safe equipment
Validity: Maximum 4 hours (renewable with re-inspection)
Issuing Authority: Safety Officer
Approving Authority: Head of Operations + Safety Manager (dual signature)

Pre-conditions for Hot Work Permit:
a) Area gas testing: LEL <10% in work area AND within 15-meter radius
b) Gas testing equipment: Calibrated multi-gas detector (LEL, H2S, O2)
c) Gas test validity: Maximum 30 minutes before work start; re-test every 2 hours during work
d) Fire watcher: Dedicated fire watcher mandatory throughout hot work duration
e) Fire extinguisher: Minimum 2x DCP 9kg extinguishers at work site
f) Fire water: Ensure fire water line pressurized and hose connected
g) Combustibles: All combustible materials removed/protected within 10-meter radius
h) Drainage: All drains covered with non-combustible material within 10-meter radius
i) Ventilation: Forced ventilation mandatory in confined/semi-confined spaces

3. CONFINED SPACE ENTRY PERMIT (CSEP)
MANDATORY for entry into any confined space (tanks, vessels, columns, pits, sewers, exchanger shells)
Validity: 8 hours
Issuing Authority: Safety Officer
Approving Authority: Safety Manager + Medical Officer sign-off

Confined Space Entry Requirements:
a) Atmosphere testing: O2: 19.5-23.5%, LEL: <10%, H2S: <5 ppm, CO: <25 ppm
b) Continuous monitoring: Multi-gas detector inside space throughout entry
c) Entry standby: Minimum 1 trained standby person at entry point — NEVER LEAVE
d) Rescue equipment: Full body harness, lifeline, SCBA set at entry
e) Communication: Radio communication maintained between entrant and standby
f) Isolation: All process connections blanked/blinded (NOT valve isolation alone)
g) Lockout/Tagout: All energy sources locked out per SOP-004

4. ELECTRICAL WORK PERMIT (EWP)
For work on electrical equipment, panels, and instrumentation.
Requires: LOTO procedure completed, area classification confirmed, insulation resistance check

PTW PROCEDURE — STEP BY STEP:

STEP 1: WORK REQUEST
- Originator (maintenance supervisor) raises Work Request in SAP PM
- Describes work scope, equipment, hazards, resources required

STEP 2: RISK ASSESSMENT
- Maintenance supervisor completes Job Safety Analysis (JSA) / Task Risk Assessment (TRA)
- Identifies: What can go wrong? How severe? How likely? What controls?
- Sign-off required from all workers performing the job

STEP 3: ISOLATION PLAN
- Operations team prepares isolation certificate
- All energy sources identified: Process fluid, electricity, pneumatic, hydraulic
- Isolation points numbered and recorded on P&ID sketch

STEP 4: PHYSICAL ISOLATION (LOTO)
- Operations engineer physically isolates equipment
- Drain, depressurize, and purge/flush as required
- Apply locks and tags to all isolation points
- Tag reads: DANGER — DO NOT OPERATE — Work Permit No. [XXX]
- Lock keys held by: Permit Issuer (1 key) + Each worker (1 key each)

STEP 5: PERMIT ISSUE
- Permit Issuer and Approving Authority jointly inspect worksite
- Verify all pre-conditions met
- Sign and issue permit — one copy at worksite, one copy at control room

STEP 6: WORK EXECUTION
- Work proceeds only after permit physically in hand at worksite
- Any change in scope: STOP work, cancel permit, issue new permit
- Any unsafe condition: Worker authority to STOP work immediately

STEP 7: PERMIT CLOSURE
- Work completed and area cleaned
- Equipment reinstated — all temporary modifications removed
- Permit Issuer inspects before closing
- Isolation removed only after permit officially closed
- Test run of equipment if required

STEP 8: SIMULTANEOUS OPERATIONS (SIMOPS)
When multiple work permits active near same area:
- SIMOPS matrix prepared — identifies conflicts
- Additional controls applied (communication, coordination)
- Safety officer to coordinate

CRITICAL DON'Ts:
❌ Never start work without permit in hand at worksite
❌ Never extend permit verbally — must be reissued in writing
❌ Never bypass LOTO to "save time"
❌ Never allow gas test to be >30 minutes old for hot work
❌ Never enter confined space without standby person
❌ Never perform hot work if LEL reading >10%

PENALTIES FOR VIOLATION:
- Any violation of PTW system = Immediate suspension pending enquiry
- Repeat violations = Termination
- Management equally responsible if they condone violations

References: OISD-105 (Work Permit), IS 15001 (Confined Space), Factory Act 1948 Section 36 (Precautions against explosive atmospheres)
