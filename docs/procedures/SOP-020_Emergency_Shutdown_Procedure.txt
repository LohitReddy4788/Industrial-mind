STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP Number: SOP-020
Title: Emergency Shutdown Procedure — Unit 3 (Crude Distillation + Hydrocracker)
Revision: 4
Effective Date: 01-March-2024
Approved By: Plant Manager, Head of Operations, Safety Manager
Classification: CRITICAL SAFETY DOCUMENT — Post at all operator stations

PURPOSE:
Define actions for orderly emergency shutdown to protect personnel, equipment, and environment during emergency conditions.

TRIGGER CONDITIONS — INITIATE EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN WHEN:
1. Fire or explosion in or near process unit
2. Major hydrocarbon or toxic gas leak (LEL >25% at gas detector, or visible large liquid release)
3. Loss of cooling water to plant (total failure >5 minutes)
4. Power failure (total blackout lasting >2 minutes)
5. Loss of instrument air (pressure <3 bar and dropping)
6. Runaway reaction indicators (temperature/pressure rising rapidly, uncontrolled)
7. H2S gas detection at >10 ppm in occupied areas
8. Structural damage to major equipment
9. Natural disaster (earthquake, flood) impacting plant
10. Operator judgment that situation poses imminent danger

ESD LEVELS:
Level 1 — Unit Emergency Shutdown: Shuts down specific process unit only
Level 2 — Plant Emergency Shutdown: Shuts down entire plant
Level 3 — Emergency Blowdown: Depressurizes all vessels to flare (used for fire scenarios)

AUTOMATED ESD SYSTEM (operates without operator action):
The following cause automatic ESD Level 1 (Unit Shutdown):
- High-high pressure on any column (P+15% above design) → ESD valve closes
- High-high temperature on any furnace coil (design + 30°C) → Fuel gas cut-off
- Low-low flow to furnace (below minimum required) → Emergency fuel cut-off
- High-high level in atmospheric column (>90%) → Feed pump trip
- Compressor vibration high-high (>15 mm/s) → Compressor trip
- Loss of flame in furnace (all pilots extinguished) → Fuel gas cut-off

MANUAL EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN PROCEDURE — OPERATOR ACTIONS:

STEP 1: SOUND ALARM (0-30 seconds)
- Press emergency siren button at nearest alarm station OR control room console
- Announce on PA system: "EMERGENCY IN UNIT 3 — ALL PERSONNEL MUSTER AT DESIGNATED ASSEMBLY POINT. THIS IS NOT A DRILL."
- Alert shift incharge immediately (radio or direct)

STEP 2: ISOLATE FEED TO UNIT (30 seconds - 2 minutes)
Control Room Actions:
☐ Close feed valve to atmospheric distillation column (FCV-001) — switch to MANUAL, set 0%
☐ Trip crude feed pumps P-101 and P-102 from DCS
☐ Close fuel gas supply to furnaces F-101, F-102 (ESDV-001, ESDV-002)
☐ Initiate column reflux rundown (maintain reflux to avoid column damage from temperature spike)
☐ Switch all pressure controllers to MANUAL to prevent runaway pressure buildup

Field Actions (done in parallel by field operators):
☐ Close block valve on crude feed line (MV-001) — located at Unit 3 battery limit
☐ Confirm furnace pilots extinguished (visual check)
☐ Close all product rundown valves to tankage (prevent contamination if upset condition)

STEP 3: EVACUATE HAZARD AREA (simultaneous with Step 2)
- Account for ALL personnel using muster roll
- Shift Incharge: Confirm headcount at assembly point
- Report any missing person to Emergency Control Centre IMMEDIATELY
- DO NOT re-enter hazard area to search — inform fire brigade with last known location

STEP 4: COMPRESSOR AND ROTATING EQUIPMENT (2-5 minutes)
☐ Trip compressors C-401, C-402, C-403 (from DCS or local panel)
☐ Trip all pumps in affected unit from MCC (use group trip if available)
☐ For H2 service compressors: Purge with N2 after trip (open N2 purge valve NV-401)

STEP 5: COOLING AND UTILITIES (3-7 minutes)
☐ Confirm cooling water flow maintained (do NOT stop CW during emergency — needed for condenser/cooler protection)
☐ If fire: Activate fire water deluge systems for exposed vessels/piping
☐ Isolate steam to non-critical users (maintain steam to steam purging systems)

STEP 6: EMERGENCY BLOWDOWN (Level 3 — for fire scenario ONLY)
Only initiated when:
- Vessel/piping involved in fire and risk of BLEVE (Boiling Liquid Expanding Vapour Explosion)
- Instruction from Plant Manager or Emergency Commander
Procedure:
☐ Open emergency blowdown valve (BDV-001) — routes high-pressure inventory to flare
☐ Depressurize at controlled rate (maximum 7 bar/minute to avoid thermal shock)
☐ Maintain flare pilot flame (check flare KO drum level)

STEP 7: COMMUNICATE AND DOCUMENT
☐ Inform plant manager and safety manager
☐ Call emergency services if required (fire brigade, ambulance) — numbers at control room
☐ Record timeline of all actions in emergency log
☐ Preserve evidence (do NOT disturb equipment before investigation)

COMPRESSOR EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN (Specific — C-401 Series):
If compressor vibration high or abnormal noise:
1. Trip from DCS immediately (do not wait for automatic trip)
2. Close suction block valve (XV-401)
3. Close discharge check valve (confirm no back-flow)
4. For H2 service: Open N2 purge (NV-401) to displace flammable gas
5. Do NOT open compressor for inspection until: Gas-free confirmed, permit issued

IMPORTANT CAUTIONS:
⚠️ Do NOT attempt to fight a major hydrocarbon fire with portable extinguishers — EVACUATE and call fire brigade
⚠️ Do NOT re-enter after evacuation without clearance from Shift Incharge + Safety Officer
⚠️ Do NOT restart equipment after emergency shutdown without formal investigation and clearance
⚠️ H2S: If H2S alarm sounds, immediately don SCBA (do not wait to smell — H2S deadens sense of smell)

POST-EMERGENCY RESTART:
No unit shall be restarted after emergency shutdown without:
1. Written clearance from Safety Manager
2. Completed root cause investigation (preliminary)
3. Inspection of all affected equipment
4. Formal startup procedure followed (per SOP-015)
5. Plant Manager approval for restart

This procedure shall be rehearsed in emergency drills: Minimum once every 6 months (unannounced drill annually)
