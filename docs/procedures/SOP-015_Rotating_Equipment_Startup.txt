STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP Number: SOP-015
Title: Rotating Equipment — Pre-Startup Checklist and Startup Procedure
Revision: 3
Effective Date: 15-January-2024
Approved By: Head of Mechanical Maintenance, Head of Operations

SCOPE: Centrifugal pumps, compressors, fans, and blowers in all process units.

MANDATORY PRE-STARTUP CHECKLIST (Complete ALL items — no exceptions):

A. MECHANICAL CHECKS:
☐ A1. Coupling guard fitted and secure — bolts tightened
☐ A2. Alignment verified (if recently maintained) — log reading
☐ A3. Shaft rotation free — manually barred over minimum 2 full rotations
☐ A4. Bearing housing oil level correct (sight glass 50-75% full) OR grease nipple check
☐ A5. All drain valves closed and plugged
☐ A6. All vent valves closed (for liquid pumps)
☐ A7. Mechanical seal: No leakage, seal support system pressurized (Plan 53B: check accumulator pre-charge)
☐ A8. All instruments connected — pressure gauges, temperature gauges, vibration probes
☐ A9. Suction strainer: Clean (check differential pressure if gauges fitted)

B. ELECTRICAL CHECKS:
☐ B1. Motor insulation resistance (if newly installed): >1 MΩ at 500V megger
☐ B2. Motor terminal box: Closed and sealed, cable entry sealed
☐ B3. Earth connection: Secure (shake test)
☐ B4. Local isolator: Ready/Auto position (confirm with electrical team)
☐ B5. MCC (Motor Control Centre): Circuit breaker in service, fuses in place

C. PROCESS/OPERATIONS CHECKS:
☐ C1. Suction block valve: OPEN
☐ C2. Discharge block valve: CLOSED (for pumps — open after startup) or CRACKED OPEN
☐ C3. Minimum flow bypass (if applicable): OPEN
☐ C4. Pressure gauges: Reading suction pressure (confirms suction valve open and line pressurized)
☐ C5. Confirm process system ready to receive flow (downstream equipment ready)
☐ C6. LOTO removed: Verify no locks or tags remaining on equipment
☐ C7. Work permit: CLOSED (confirm with permit issuer)
☐ C8. No personnel working on or near equipment

D. SAFETY CHECKS:
☐ D1. Equipment area: Clear of all tools, scaffolding, temporary equipment
☐ D2. Fire detection system: Active (check with safety room)
☐ D3. Gas detectors in equipment area: Showing 0% LEL
☐ D4. Eye wash station: Accessible and in working condition
☐ D5. Emergency stop (E-STOP): Test function (bump test) before startup

STARTUP PROCEDURE — CENTRIFUGAL PUMP:

Step 1: Prime the pump
- For liquid service: Open vent valve on casing. Fill pump with process fluid until liquid exits vent.
- Close vent valve. Confirm no air pockets (re-vent if discharge pressure low after start).
- For hot service (>150°C): Allow 30-minute warm-up with casing drain cracked open to prevent thermal shock.

Step 2: Check seal flush
- Verify seal flush flow (Plan 11/13): Flow indicator showing flow.
- For Plan 53B: Verify accumulator pre-charge 2-3 bar above max seal chamber pressure.
- No leaks from seal area before starting.

Step 3: Start motor
- From DCS or local panel as per operating instruction.
- Observe starting current (should return to normal within 5-8 seconds).
- If motor fails to start or current does not drop: Stop immediately, investigate before retry.

Step 4: Immediate post-start checks (within 2 minutes of start):
- Motor current: Check against nameplate FLA (should be 90-110% of expected running current)
- Discharge pressure: Rising as expected
- Bearing temperatures: Both DE and NDE — should stabilize within 20-30 minutes
- Vibration: Listen for abnormal noise; check vibration monitor reading
- Seal: No leakage
- Suction pressure: Stable (not falling — would indicate cavitation/low level)

Step 5: Open discharge valve gradually
- Open discharge block valve slowly over 1-2 minutes
- Watch for cavitation (unusual noise, vibration spike, suction pressure drop)
- Bring flow to required rate per operating target

Step 6: Stabilization monitoring (first 30 minutes):
- Record readings every 5 minutes: Suction P, Discharge P, Flow, Motor current, Bearing temp
- Any reading outside limits: Stop pump, investigate

ABNORMAL STARTUP CONDITIONS — ACTION:

High Vibration (>7.1 mm/s) immediately after start:
→ Stop pump. Check coupling alignment. Check for cavitation. Check for rubbing (barring difficulty).

Motor does not start / trips on overcurrent:
→ Check voltage at terminals (should be 415V ±6%). Check for mechanical binding (bar over). Check starter.

No/Low flow after start:
→ Check suction valve open. Check for vapor lock (re-prime). Check rotation direction (reversed phase).

Seal leaking on startup:
→ Stop pump if leak exceeds drip rate. DO NOT run with significant seal leakage. Call maintenance.

High bearing temperature (>85°C within first hour):
→ Stop pump. Check oil level. Check oil quality (contamination). Check alignment.

STARTUP AFTER MAINTENANCE:
Additional checks required:
- Alignment certificate reviewed (if shaft/coupling work done)
- Torque check on all bolted joints (record in work completion report)
- Mechanical seal installation certificate reviewed
- Test run on water/low-hazard fluid before process startup (if feasible)
- Gradual load increase — minimum 30-minute light-load run before full process load

DOCUMENTATION:
- Complete all checklist items with initials
- Record pre-startup readings in Equipment Log Book
- Startup time and person to be recorded in DCS historian
- Any deviations from normal: Record in shift log and inform supervisor
