DGMS (DIRECTORATE GENERAL OF MINES SAFETY) — SAFETY REGULATIONS
Relevant Extracts for Petroleum Refinery and Chemical Plant Operations

Note: While DGMS primarily governs mines, many petroleum installations (especially those with gas wells, exploration) fall under dual DGMS/OISD jurisdiction. This document covers applicable sections.

SECTION A — MACHINERY SAFETY REGULATIONS

A.1 Guards and Safety Devices (Mines Regulations 1955, Regulation 99-105):
All rotating machinery must have:
- Guards on all exposed moving parts (couplings, belt drives, chain drives, fan blades)
- Guard must be designed so it CANNOT be removed while machine is running
- Guard material: Steel mesh or solid sheet minimum (wood NOT acceptable in hazardous areas)
- Guard must be maintained in good repair — damaged guards must be repaired before machine restart

A.2 Operator Responsibilities:
- NO operator shall work on moving machinery (adjust, clean, lubricate) unless machine is stopped AND isolated
- Oiling of rotating bearings: ONLY if oil point is safely accessible from outside of guard
- If in doubt about safety: STOP machine, report to supervisor

SECTION B — ELECTRICAL SAFETY

B.1 Earthing Requirements:
All electrical equipment frames must be earthed. Earth resistance: <1 Ohm
Earth continuity checks: Monthly by electrical department

B.2 Working on Electrical Equipment:
- Work on live equipment: PROHIBITED (except testing with approved test equipment by authorized person)
- Minimum isolation: Two points of isolation with locks applied
- Voltage testing before work: Mandatory using approved voltage indicator

B.3 High Voltage (>1000V):
- Only authorized electrical supervisors may work
- Earthing sticks mandatory to discharge capacitance
- Minimum two-person rule (no solo HV work)

SECTION C — FIRE AND EXPLOSION PREVENTION

C.1 Hot Work in Hazardous Areas:
Requirements (aligned with OISD-105):
1. Written permit from manager (equivalent to PTW Hot Work Permit)
2. Gas test immediately before starting hot work: <20% LEL in work area
   (Note: DGMS uses 20% LEL threshold vs OISD 10% — always use the more stringent OISD 10%)
3. Re-test every 2 hours during hot work
4. Fire watch: Present throughout and for minimum 30 minutes after work completion
5. All flammable material removed or protected within 10-meter radius
6. Emergency equipment (fire extinguisher, water hose) at worksite

C.2 Prohibited Items in Hazardous Areas:
- Matches, lighters, non-intrinsically safe mobile phones/cameras
- Smoking: PROHIBITED within plant fence line (zero tolerance)
- Non-Zone-rated electrical equipment
- Open footwear (sandals) — safety shoes mandatory

SECTION D — HEALTH AND PPE REQUIREMENTS

D.1 Minimum PPE Requirements in Process Plant:
Standard Working in Plant:
- Safety helmet (IS 2925 certified)
- Safety shoes with steel toe cap and anti-static (IS 15298 certified)
- Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1 or IS 5983)
- Fire-retardant clothing (FRC) in hydrocarbon areas

Additional for Specific Work:
- Working with acids/caustics: Chemical splash goggles + face shield + chemical-resistant gloves + apron
- Working with H2S risk areas: Calibrated personal H2S detector mandatory (alarm at 5 ppm, evacuate at 10 ppm)
- Working at height (>1.8 meters): Full body harness + double lanyard + helmet with chinstrap
- Welding: Welding shield, leather gloves, leather apron, safety shoes
- Noise areas (>85 dB): Ear protection (ear plugs or muffs) — mandatory

D.2 Medical Requirements:
- All plant workers: Annual medical fitness examination
- Workers in H2S, benzene, or lead service: 6-monthly blood tests (CBC, liver function)
- First aid trained persons: Minimum 1 per shift per 50 workers

SECTION E — ACCIDENT REPORTING AND INVESTIGATION

E.1 Reporting Requirements:
Fatal Accidents: Report to DGMS within 24 hours; Preliminary report within 7 days
Serious Bodily Injury (fractures, burns >10% body, hospitalization): Report within 24 hours
Dangerous Occurrences (explosions, fire, structural collapse): Report within 24 hours

E.2 Investigation:
- Plant management investigation: Begin within 24 hours
- Root cause report: Within 30 days
- Corrective actions: Implemented with target dates

SECTION F — INSPECTION OF PRESSURE VESSELS AND BOILERS

F.1 All pressure vessels in plant: Subject to Factories Act 1948 Section 31 and relevant State Factory Rules
F.2 Inspection authority: Inspector of Factories (for Factories Act sites)
F.3 Certificate validity: As per statutory inspection report (typically 3 years)
F.4 Operation without valid certificate: Illegal — operators/managers liable for prosecution
