OISD STANDARD 118 — KEY REQUIREMENTS SUMMARY
==============================================
Standard: OISD-118 (Oil Industry Safety Directorate)
Title: Layouts for Oil and Gas Installations
Applicability: Petroleum refineries, oil storage terminals, LPG plants, pipelines
Mandatory for: All petroleum sector installations in India under PESO/MoPNG jurisdiction

SECTION 6 — ROTATING EQUIPMENT

Section 6.1 — General Requirements:
All rotating equipment in hydrocarbon service shall:
(a) Be designed, installed, and maintained per API standards (API-610 for pumps)
(b) Have vibration monitoring capability where process criticality demands
(c) Not be operated above design limits — speed, temperature, pressure, flow
(d) Have mechanical seals with appropriate flush plans per API-682

Section 6.2 — Centrifugal Pumps:
6.2.1 Minimum Flow Protection:
All centrifugal pumps shall be protected against operation below minimum continuous safe flow.
Where automatic minimum flow bypass is not provided, operating procedures must clearly define
minimum flow requirements and consequences of low-flow operation.
NOTE: Manual monitoring alone is NOT considered adequate protection for pumps in hazardous service.

6.2.2 Mechanical Seal Requirements:
(a) All pumps in flammable hydrocarbon service shall use API-682 compliant mechanical seals
(b) Seal flush system (API Plan) must be maintained in serviceable condition at all times
(c) Seal flush flow rate must be verified during each maintenance turnaround
(d) Dry-run protection (dry-run detector or minimum flow protection) is MANDATORY for
    single mechanical seals in Category 1 service (high pressure, high temperature, toxic, or flammable)
(e) Dual seals: backup seal must be tested at every planned maintenance to confirm integrity

6.2.3 Vibration Limits:
Normal operating vibration: < 5.0 mm/s RMS
Alert level: 5.0 – 7.5 mm/s RMS (investigate cause)
Danger level: > 7.5 mm/s RMS (shutdown required)
Action time after danger alarm: immediate (within 15 minutes)

6.2.4 Bearing Temperature Limits:
Normal: < 60°C above ambient
Alert: 60-70°C above ambient
Danger: > 70°C above ambient (shutdown required)

SECTION 12 — PRESSURE VESSELS

Section 12.1 — General:
All pressure vessels shall be designed, fabricated, tested, and operated per IBR (Indian Boiler Regulations),
ASME Section VIII, or equivalent national standard. Vessels operating above 1 barg require
a written scheme of examination under Factory Act Section 31.

Section 12.2 — Safe Operating Limits:
No pressure vessel shall be operated:
(a) Above its design pressure
(b) Above its design temperature
(c) Below its design minimum temperature (to avoid brittle fracture)
Operating procedures must specify all three limits clearly and conspicuously at the operating station.

Section 12.3 — Overpressure Protection:
12.3.1 Every pressure vessel shall have at least one pressure safety valve (PSV) set at or below MAWP.
12.3.2 PSV shall be sized to prevent pressure rising more than 10% above MAWP under maximum relieving load.
12.3.3 PSV discharge shall be piped to a safe location (flare or safe vent) — NEVER to atmosphere near personnel.
12.3.4 CRITICALLY: No isolation valve shall be placed between a pressure vessel and its PSV unless
    a car-sealed open (CSO) block valve arrangement is provided.

Section 12.4 — Inspection and Testing of Safety Valves:
(a) All PSVs shall be tested and recertified at intervals not exceeding 12 months.
(b) Test records must be maintained and available for inspection.
(c) Where PSV testing is overdue: vessel shall NOT be operated until PSV is tested and certified.
(d) PSV lifting test must be conducted on a certified test bench — field testing not acceptable
    except for initial set-pressure verification.

Section 12.5 — Statutory Inspection:
External inspection: minimum every 2 years by competent person
Internal inspection: minimum every 4 years or as per fitness-for-service methodology
Remaining life < 2 years: notify Competent Authority and accelerate inspection schedule

SECTION 18 — INCIDENT REPORTING

Section 18.1 — Significant Process Safety Events:
The following must be reported to PESO (Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation) within 24 hours:
- Any PSV lifting due to overpressure (other than controlled test)
- Any fire lasting more than 5 minutes or with damage
- Any explosion
- Any fatality or serious injury
- Any uncontrolled release of hydrocarbons > 5 tonnes

Section 18.2 — Root Cause Analysis:
For Severity High or above: formal root cause analysis (RCA) using RCFA methodology required within 30 days.
RCA report to be submitted to OISD if event resulted in fatality or major damage.

SECTION 4 — PERMIT TO WORK SYSTEM

Section 4.1 — Requirements:
A formal Permit to Work (PTW) system is mandatory for all maintenance and non-routine operations.
Hot Work Permit required for: welding, grinding, cutting, any ignition source in hazardous area.
Confined Space Entry Permit required for: entry into any vessel, tank, pit, trench, or enclosed space.

Section 4.2 — PTW Issuer Requirements:
PTW must be issued by Area Authority (designated role) — not by maintenance personnel.
PTW must be signed and displayed at the work location throughout the job.
PTW cancellation: immediately if conditions change or hazard detected.

END OF OISD-118 SUMMARY
