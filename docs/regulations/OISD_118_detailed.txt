OISD STANDARD 118 — LAYOUT FOR OIL AND GAS INSTALLATIONS
(Oil Industry Safety Directorate — Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas, India)
Summary of Key Requirements for Plant Safety and Layout

SECTION 1 — GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

1.1 Applicability:
All new onshore petroleum/gas processing plants, refineries, petrochemical plants, and LPG bottling plants under OISD jurisdiction. Also serves as guideline for plant modification and expansion.

1.2 Hazard Classification — Petroleum Products (as per Petroleum Act 1934):
Class A: Flash point below 23°C (e.g., Naphtha, Petrol, LPG, Hydrogen)
Class B: Flash point 23°C to 65°C (e.g., Kerosene, Diesel, Jet Fuel ATF)
Class C: Flash point above 65°C (e.g., Furnace Oil, Bitumen)

SECTION 2 — MINIMUM SEPARATION DISTANCES (Key Requirements)

2.1 Between Process Units:
- Minimum distance between adjacent process units: 30 meters
- For units handling Class A products: 45 meters minimum
- Distance from process unit to property boundary: 60 meters minimum
- Distance from process unit to public road: 45 meters

2.2 Fired Heaters / Furnaces:
- Fired heater to pump/compressor handling flammable fluid: MINIMUM 30 meters
- Fired heater to Class A storage tank: 45 meters
- Fired heater to battery limit of plot: 30 meters
CRITICAL: Any hydrocarbon leak within 30m of fired heater is a MAJOR FIRE RISK

2.3 Rotating Equipment (Pumps, Compressors):
- Pump house to process column/vessel: Minimum 15 meters
- Compressor house to fired equipment: Minimum 30 meters
- Compressor house to administration building: 60 meters

SECTION 3 — FIRE PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Fire Water System:
- Fire water ring main pressure: Minimum 7 kg/cm² (at hydraulically most remote point)
- Fire water flow rate: Calculated per OISD-116 fire scenario, minimum 2000 LPM available at any one point
- Fire hydrant spacing: Maximum 30 meters apart in process areas
- Fire hydrant spacing: Maximum 45 meters apart in storage areas
- Fire pump capacity: 100% duty + 100% standby (auto-start on pressure drop to 6.5 kg/cm²)
- Jockey pump: Maintain system pressure 7.5 kg/cm²

3.2 Fixed Fire Protection Systems Required:
For Class A/B Hydrocarbon Pumps in enclosed pump houses:
- Automatic sprinkler OR deluge system mandatory
- Gas detection system (LEL detectors): 2-out-of-3 logic for deluge activation
- Foam pouring system for pump sumps (if hydrocarbon collection likely)

For LPG / High-Pressure Gas Compressor houses:
- Automatic gas detection + deluge water curtain mandatory
- Water spray for vessel/piping cooling at 10.2 L/min/m² surface area

3.3 Fire Extinguisher Requirements:
Process areas: DCP 9 kg type at maximum 15-meter travel distance
Electrical panels/control rooms: CO2 type (NOT DCP)
Pump houses: Foam trolley (50L) + DCP extinguishers minimum

SECTION 4 — ELECTRICAL AREA CLASSIFICATION

4.1 Zone Classification for Hazardous Areas (as per IS 5572/IEC 60079):
Zone 0: Explosive atmosphere present continuously or for long periods
- Inside open-top tanks, inside process vessels (when open)
Zone 1: Explosive atmosphere likely to occur in normal operation
- Pump seal area within 3-meter radius
- Compressor area within 3-meter radius
- Open drain sumps within 1.5-meter radius
- Vent/relief valve outlets within 3-meter radius (horizontal) × 1-meter (vertical)
Zone 2: Explosive atmosphere not likely but may occur in abnormal conditions
- Beyond Zone 1 boundary up to 8 meters from release point
- Enclosed pump rooms (general area)
- Cable trenches in process areas

4.2 Equipment Selection for Hazardous Areas:
Zone 0: Intrinsically Safe (Ex ia) equipment ONLY
Zone 1: Flame-proof (Ex d), Increased Safety (Ex e), or Intrinsically Safe (Ex ia/ib)
Zone 2: Any of Zone 1 types OR Non-sparking (Ex n)

CRITICAL: Using non-rated electrical equipment in hazardous zone = violation of Factories Act Section 36 and OISD-113

SECTION 5 — VIBRATION LIMITS FOR ROTATING EQUIPMENT

Per OISD-118 and ISO 10816-3:
Pumps and Compressors:
Zone A (New equipment acceptable): <2.3 mm/s RMS
Zone B (Continue operation acceptable): 2.3 to 4.5 mm/s RMS
Zone C (Alarm — investigate): 4.5 to 7.1 mm/s RMS
Zone D (DANGER — shutdown): >7.1 mm/s RMS

Vibration monitoring requirements:
- All pumps >75 kW: Online vibration monitoring required (OISD-118 Para 6.2.4)
- Compressors: Continuous online monitoring mandatory regardless of size
- Manual monitoring interval (where online not available): Maximum 30-day intervals for critical equipment

SECTION 6 — PRESSURE RELIEF DEVICE REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Every pressure vessel, heat exchanger, and process column MUST have:
- PSV (Pressure Safety Valve) sized for maximum credible overpressure scenario
- PSV set pressure ≤ design pressure of vessel
- PSV discharge: To closed flare header (NOT to atmosphere for toxic/flammable service)

6.2 PSV Testing Intervals:
- Normal service: Every 3 years (maximum)
- Severe/corrosive service: Every 1-2 years
- PSV testing authority: Third-party Inspection Body (TPI) approved by CCOE/PESO

6.3 Pressure Vessel Inspection (as per Factories Act Rule 61):
- First inspection: Before commissioning
- Routine inspection: Every 3 years (external), 6 years (internal)
- Thickness measurement: Every inspection by ultrasonic testing (UT)
- Inspection report: Maintained and produced on demand to Factory Inspector

SECTION 7 — EMERGENCY SYSTEMS

7.1 Emergency Shutdown System (ESD):
- Plants with flammable/toxic inventory >10 tonnes: Automated ESD mandatory
- ESD must be independent of DCS (separate system)
- ESD testing: Full test every 2 years; partial stroke test every 6 months
- ESD response time: Actuate within 5 seconds of signal

7.2 Emergency Communication:
- Public Address (PA) system: Audible throughout plant at 85 dB minimum above background noise
- Emergency siren: Distinct from other alarms; audible 1 km radius
- Emergency contact numbers posted at all entry points and control room

References: Petroleum Act 1934, Petroleum Rules 2002, Factories Act 1948, PESO regulations, IS 5572, IS 15001
