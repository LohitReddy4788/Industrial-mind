THE FACTORIES ACT, 1948
RELEVANT SECTIONS FOR PETROLEUM INDUSTRY OPERATIONS
=====================================================
Source: Ministry of Labour and Employment, Government of India
Applicable to: All factories registered under the Factories Act, including petroleum refineries

SECTION 31 — PRESSURE PLANT

31(1) If in any factory, any part of the plant or machinery used in a manufacturing process is
operated at a pressure above atmospheric pressure, effective measures shall be taken to ensure
that the safe working pressure of such part is not exceeded.

31(2) The State Government may make rules providing for the examination and testing of any plant
or machinery such as is referred to in sub-section (1) and prescribing such other safety measures
in relation thereto as may in its opinion be necessary in the interests of the safety of the workers.

INTERPRETATION AND APPLICATION:
- All pressure vessels operating above atmospheric pressure are covered
- "Effective measures" includes PSV installation, operating limits, inspection, and trained operators
- Written Scheme of Examination (WSE) is required for all pressure plant
- Annual statutory inspection by Inspecting Authority (or their approved representative) required
- WSE must be reviewed and renewed at intervals specified by competent authority (typically 14 months max)
- Operating companies must maintain pressure plant inspection records available for 7 years

ENFORCEMENT: Factory Inspectors of the State Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health (DISH)
have authority to inspect, issue prohibition notices, and prosecute violations.

SECTION 40-B — SAFETY OFFICERS

40-B: In every factory where (a) one thousand or more workers are ordinarily employed, or
(b) the manufacturing process involves the risk of bodily injury, poisoning, or disease — the
occupier shall, if so required by the State Government by notification in the Official Gazette,
appoint such number of Safety Officers with such qualifications and duties as may be prescribed.

For petroleum refineries: Safety Officer appointment is MANDATORY irrespective of employee count
(Schedule I industry under Factories Act — inherently hazardous).

SECTION 41-A — SITE APPRAISAL COMMITTEE

For hazardous processes (Schedule I industries including petroleum): A Site Appraisal Committee
comprising representatives from the factory, local authority, and state government shall be
constituted. Committee must be consulted before any expansion of hazardous operations.

SECTION 41-B — COMPULSORY DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION

The occupier shall disclose all information regarding:
(a) Hazards of the manufacturing process
(b) Safety measures required for control of such hazards
...to workers and adjacent communities. This disclosure must be made annually.

SECTION 41-C — SPECIFIC RESPONSIBILITY OF THE OCCUPIER

Where hazardous substances are used:
(a) The occupier shall maintain accurate and up-to-date health records of all workers
(b) Appoint a Safety Officer experienced in hazard control
(c) Provide workers with training on hazards and protective measures
(d) Prepare an on-site emergency plan and coordinate with local authorities for off-site plan

SECTION 41-H — RIGHT OF WORKERS TO WARN ABOUT IMMINENT DANGER

Any worker who has reasonable apprehension that there is an imminent danger to life or health
may bring the same to the notice of the occupier, manager, or safety officer and shall not
be dismissed or otherwise prejudiced for doing so.

IMPLICATION FOR MAINTENANCE: Maintenance personnel who raise safety concerns about equipment
condition are protected under this section. Suppression of safety concerns by management
constitutes a violation of the Factories Act.

SECTION 92 — PENALTIES

Violation of the Factories Act: Fine up to Rs 2 lakh and/or imprisonment up to 2 years.
If violation results in death: Fine up to Rs 3 lakh and/or imprisonment up to 7 years.
Continuing violation (after conviction): Additional fine of up to Rs 1000 per day.

SCHEDULE I — HAZARDOUS PROCESSES (relevant extract):
The following manufacturing processes are declared hazardous under Section 2(cb):
Item 29: Operations involving use of petroleum and its products, including refining
Item 30: Operations involving use of dangerous petroleum gases
Item 31: Manufacture and handling of explosives

NOTE: Petroleum refineries operating under Schedule I are subject to enhanced inspection
frequency, mandatory Safety Committee, mandatory on-site emergency plan, and mandatory
disclosure to workers. DISH inspection may be conducted without advance notice.

SELECTED STATE-SPECIFIC RULES (Gujarat Factories Rules, 1963):
Rule 68A: Every pressure vessel shall be examined at least once in every 12 months by a
competent person — this is MORE STRINGENT than the central Factories Act requirement.
Rule 68B: All pressure relief devices shall be tested at least once in 12 months.
Rule 68C: Records of all pressure plant examinations shall be kept for a minimum of 10 years.
