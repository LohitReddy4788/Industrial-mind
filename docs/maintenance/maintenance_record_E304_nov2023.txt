MAINTENANCE WORK ORDER RECORD
==============================
Work Order: WO-2023-1147
Equipment ID: E-304
Equipment Name: Crude Preheat Heat Exchanger (Shell & Tube)
Location: Unit-2, Preheat Train B
Date of Maintenance: 22-Nov-2023
Maintenance Type: Corrective — Fouling and Performance Degradation

Technician: Ramesh Pillai (Senior Maintenance Technician)
Supervised by: Suresh Nair (Lead Maintenance Engineer)
Approved by: Priya Menon (Reliability Engineer)

WORK DESCRIPTION:
E-304 performance degradation reported by operations — crude preheat temperature dropped from
design 165°C to 138°C over past 6 weeks. Duty recovery: originally 4.2 MW, current 2.8 MW.
Differential pressure across exchanger increased from 1.1 barg to 3.4 barg — indicative of
significant tube-side fouling. Decision made to pull and clean.

WORK PERFORMED:
1. Exchanger isolated — shell-side (hot crude) and tube-side (water) block valves closed.
2. Pressure relieved both sides — vented to closed drain system.
3. Shell head removed (Craftsman: Pillai, K. Murthy).
4. Bundle pulled using extraction tool — bundle weight approx. 2.4 tonnes.
5. Tube inspection:
   - Heavy crude fouling on outer surface (shell side) — asphaltene deposits, avg 4mm thick.
   - Tube inner surface (tube side): light scaling.
   - Bundle inspection found 7 tubes plugged (out of 148 total — 4.7% plugged).
   - Maximum acceptable plugging for E-304 per design: 15% — within acceptable limit.
6. High-pressure water jetting (Karcher unit, 300 bar): cleaned both shell and tube sides.
7. Post-cleaning tube inspection: all remaining 141 tubes clear, no new damage found.
8. 7 plugged tubes remained plugged (pre-existing plugs from previous turnaround TU-2021).
9. Bundle reinserted. Shell head torqued per Appendix A.
10. Pressure test: 12 barg (hydrotest) — no leaks found.
11. Returned to service: crude preheat temperature reached 161°C within 2 hours (design 165°C).

OBSERVATIONS AND FINDINGS:
- Fouling rate significantly higher than design: design fouling factor 0.00017 m²K/W (TEMA-R),
  observed fouling corresponding to approximately 0.00043 m²K/W — 2.5x design value.
- High fouling rate attributed to heavier crude slate being processed since Q3-2023.
- Fouling is primarily asphaltene deposition — consistent with crude API gravity < 22.
- Previous work order (WO-2021-0887) noted same fouling pattern. Interval between cleanings
  historically 18-24 months. With new heavy crude: interval may shorten to 9-12 months.
- Tube plugging pattern: plugged tubes all in bottom two rows of bundle — consistent with
  settled sediment and heavy asphaltene accumulation in low velocity zones.
- Corrosion: no active corrosion found. Wall thickness measurements acceptable (all > 2.6mm vs
  minimum required 2.1mm). Estimated remaining life without additional corrosion: >10 years.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE:
- Maintenance conducted per TEMA-R standards for heat exchanger maintenance.
- Pressure test per ASME PCC-1 requirements.
- Confined space entry not required (bundle external cleaning).
- PTW: Cold Work Permit CWP-2023-1089 issued, valid 22-Nov-2023.

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. IMMEDIATE: Increase E-304 cleaning frequency to 9-12 months given current crude slate.
   Trigger: differential pressure > 2.5 barg OR duty drop > 0.8 MW from post-clean baseline.
2. SHORT TERM: Evaluate online fouling monitoring (Clamp-on DP transmitter, DPT-E304) for
   continuous trending — currently only manual readings twice weekly.
3. MEDIUM TERM: Consider anti-fouling insert (Tube Turbulators) for high-fouling tubes in
   lower bundle rows to reduce deposition velocity.
4. LONG TERM: When next opportunity arises, review bundle design — current TEMA-R bundle
   may not be optimum for heavy crude service. Consider spiral corrugated tubes or twisted tape inserts.

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
Primary failure mode: Fouling (process-side)
Failure mechanism: Asphaltene deposition from heavy crude
Equipment criticality: High (preheat train — energy efficiency and crude capacity impact)
Reliability classification: Wear/aging, predictable, manageable by cleaning frequency
