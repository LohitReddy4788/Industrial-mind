=====================================
FAILURE EVENT — October 2023
Date: 08-Oct-2023
Work Order: WO-2023-0589
Type: Emergency Corrective — Unplanned Shutdown
Failure Mode: Stage-2 Discharge Valve Failure causing gas leak
Severity: HIGH — Plant Safety Incident (Minor)

Failure Description:
C-401 tripped on high discharge temperature (Stage 2: 189°C vs trip setpoint 180°C).
Subsequent inspection revealed:
- Stage-2 discharge valve (plate type) had broken valve plate (cast iron fatigue fracture)
- Broken fragments entered cylinder causing scoring on cylinder wall (Grade 3 damage)
- Piston ring on Stage-2 cylinder showed blow-by (worn rings)
- Small gas leak at Stage-2 discharge valve cover joint (minor fugitive emission)

Root Cause:
1. Valve plate fatigue failure — valve had run 26,400 hours (OEM replacement interval: 24,000 hours — OVERDUE)
2. Operating at 104% design flow for past 6 months (process demand increase)
3. Liquid carryover events from KO drum (3 events recorded in logbook — June, August, September 2023) accelerated wear

Contributing Factors:
- PM overdue by approximately 3 months (scheduled Oct vs actual Oct — but valve not replaced in last PM)
- Inadequate KO drum level control (instrument department issue)

Incident Classification: Near-Miss (potential for major gas leak averted by quick trip response)
OISD-116 reporting: Filed incident report IR-2023-047

Actions Taken:
1. Emergency isolation — shut all block valves, purged with N2
2. PTW issued (Hot Work Permit + Confined Space) for cylinder opening
3. Stage-2 discharge valves (suction + discharge, both cylinders) replaced — all 4 valves
4. Stage-2 cylinder inspected — honed to remove scoring (wall within limits after honing)
5. Stage-2 piston rings replaced (all 4 stages rings replaced preventively)
6. Piston rod packing replaced (Stage 2)
7. KO drum level instrument recalibrated and high-level alarm setpoint reduced
8. Valve cover joint re-gasketted with Flexitallic spiral wound gasket

Parts Used:
- Discharge valves (4 sets): ₹2,84,000
- Piston rings (all stages): ₹1,12,000
- Piston rod packing: ₹38,000
- Gaskets and seals: ₹22,000
Total Maintenance Cost: ₹4,56,000
Production Loss (18 hours downtime × ₹8.5L/hr): ₹1,53,00,000
Total Impact: ₹1,57,56,000

Post-Repair Performance Test:
- All stage temperatures normal (Stage 1: 142°C, Stage 2: 156°C, Stage 3: 168°C, Stage 4: 172°C)
- Discharge pressure: 184.2 bar(g) (within spec)
- Flow: 11,850 Nm³/hr (98.75% of rated)
- No gas leaks detected (soap bubble test + ultrasonic leak detection)
- Vibration: All stages within ISO 10816 limits

Lessons Learned:
1. Valve replacement intervals must be strictly followed — calendar-based PM inadequate for high-load operation
2. Overload operation requires proportional reduction in PM intervals (OEM confirmed: at 104% load, valve life reduces by ~20%)
3. KO drum liquid level management critical for valve life

Recommendations:
- Reduce valve replacement interval to 20,000 hours or annual (whichever earlier)
- Install online valve monitoring system (acoustic emission monitoring)
- Process team to maintain flow within 100% design capacity
- Monthly KO drum inspection and drain check
