INCIDENT REPORT — GAS LEAK FROM COMPRESSOR C-401
Incident Reference: IR-2023-047
Date: 08-October-2023, 14:22 hrs (Day Shift)
Location: Compressor House — Hydrocracker Unit, Bay 3
Equipment: C-401 (Reciprocating Compressor, H2-rich gas service)
Classification: Process Safety Incident — Level 2 (Significant)
Shift Incharge: Mr. Deepak Nair
Reported By: Operator Mr. Suresh Babu

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
C-401 tripped automatically on High Stage-2 Discharge Temperature (189°C vs trip at 180°C). During investigation of the trip, maintenance team opened the Stage-2 valve cover and found a broken discharge valve plate. A small gas leak (H2-rich gas, flammable) was present at the valve cover joint prior to opening. LEL detector showed 12% LEL at compressor skid level (1.5 meters elevation) at time of discovery.

CONSEQUENCES:
- Flammable gas release: Estimated 45 kg H2-rich gas (below OISD reportable threshold of 50 kg — reported voluntarily)
- No ignition, no fire
- No injuries to personnel
- Area evacuated for 90 minutes while ventilation cleared gas
- Production impact: 18 hours downtime, hydrocracker unit rate reduced by 35% during period

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE:
14:22 — C-401 trips on high temperature (automatic)
14:25 — Operator discovers gas smell at compressor house
14:26 — LEL reading confirmed at 12% — EVACUATE compressor house immediately
14:27 — Shift Incharge activates emergency response
14:28 — Fire tender stationed at compressor house entrance
14:30 — All personnel evacuated, access restricted
14:35 — Safety officer confirms with fixed gas detection (GD-401A/B/C): Peak reading 18% LEL
14:45 — Forced ventilation fans started (4 × 5000 m³/hr capacity)
15:15 — LEL confirmed <10% — maintenance team enters with SCBA for assessment
16:00 — Nature of failure confirmed: Broken valve plate, valve cover leak
16:30 — PTW issued for Stage-2 valve replacement work (Hot Work Permit NOT required — no ignition source)
08-Oct 22:00 — Repair completed, leak-testing done
09-Oct 08:15 — C-401 restarted after successful test run

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary Cause: Stage-2 discharge valve plate fatigue fracture
- Material: Cast iron plate (OEM supplied)
- Running hours at failure: 26,400 hours
- OEM replacement interval: 24,000 hours — OVERDUE by 2,400 hours
- Valve plate fractured due to combined fatigue + impact from 3 liquid carryover events (June, Aug, Sep 2023)

Secondary Cause: Inadequate PM compliance
- Valve replacement task was in PM schedule but was DEFERRED 3 times (Jun, Aug, Sep 2023)
- Deferral approvals: Signed by production supervisor (avoid shutdown during high-production period)
- No risk assessment done for PM deferral — should have triggered compensatory measures

Contributing Factors:
1. Operation at 104% rated capacity for 6 months (to meet production targets)
2. KO drum (knockout drum) level control malfunction — led to liquid carryover to compressor (3 events)
3. KO drum LIC (Level Indicating Controller) — found to have 15% calibration error (not caught in last calibration audit)

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE STATUS:
- OISD-116: Incident registered and reported to OISD Regional Office
- Factories Act Section 88A: Voluntary report to Factory Inspector (dangerous occurrence)
- Insurance notification: Filed within 24 hours per policy requirement

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:

Immediate:
✅ Replaced all Stage-2 valves (suction + discharge, both throws)
✅ Replaced piston rings (all 4 stages — preventive)
✅ KO drum level instrument recalibrated, new setpoints validated
✅ High-level alarm added on KO drum (was not present before)

Short-term (within 2 weeks):
✅ Revised valve replacement PM frequency: Every 20,000 hours OR 18 months (whichever earlier)
✅ Added valve impact monitor (acoustic emission sensors) to Stage 2 and Stage 3
✅ PM deferral policy updated: Any safety-critical PM deferral requires safety manager sign-off + written risk assessment
✅ All similar compressors (C-402, C-403) valve hours checked — C-402 at 21,500 hours, valve replacement scheduled
✅ KO drum drain procedure updated: Auto-drain every 4 hours vs previous 8 hours

Long-term (within 3 months):
✅ Valve condition monitoring system (online acoustic emission) installed on all 4 compressors
✅ Liquid carryover detection system (dP across KO drum mesh pad) installed
✅ Training conducted: All production supervisors on PM deferral risk and OISD-116 requirements

FINANCIAL IMPACT:
Emergency maintenance: ₹4,56,000
Production loss (18 hrs × hydrocracker rate): ₹1,53,00,000
Preventive repairs on C-402: ₹2,18,000
Monitoring system installation: ₹12,50,000
Investigation and reporting costs: ₹1,85,000
Total: ₹1,74,09,000

KEY LEARNING FOR INDUSTRY:
1. PM deferrals on safety-critical tasks require formal risk assessment — production pressure cannot override safety
2. Liquid carryover events are leading indicators of valve damage — must trigger immediate maintenance action
3. Overload operation (>100% design) reduces equipment life — PM intervals must be recalculated
4. Compressor valve failures give warning through high discharge temperature — temperature trending is critical
